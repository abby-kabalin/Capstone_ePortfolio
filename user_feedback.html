User Feedback

Hierarchy of Users

Method

Our team has gathered feedback from stakeholders and users involved. We have interviewed experts in data visualization along with the main users of our dashboard, ITRR and Montana Non-profit. We sent out a standard data visualization and interaction survey to all stakeholders and users as well as posted the QR code randomly throughout our local area.

Sample Questions

Some sample questions from our feedback sessions are included below:

  • “How likely are you to use data for decision making with your organization?”
  • “If you had a data visualization (graphs, charts, etc.) to use versus a static spreadsheet/table, for example, would that make you more or less likely to use data in decision-making?”
  • “If you could compare your organization/community to others that are similar, how beneficial would that be?”
Key Feedback

1. Users wanted a “One Page Report Card” based layout.
Users want to be able to see all of their data in one place. This is to ensure that the flow of the site is simple and allows for users that only need a specific visual to find it quickly and easily.

2. Users also would like the ability for Year-by-Year comparisons of all data selections to help create goals.
Users want to be able to compare data from different years to see how they have improved or declined over time. This will help them set goals for the future and allows legislatures to see the impact of the grant on these specific communities.

3. Users want a detailed narrative when results are calculated.
Clear and concise to remove misunderstanding of the visual and avoid drawing conclusions with the results. The goal of the dashboard is not to analyze the data, but simply present it in a way that is clear and easy to understand.
