Design Critiques

After producing our high-fidelity prototype, we wanted to observe how our student colleagues would perform a set of tasks using our prototype. Specifically, we aimed to identify any questions, issues, or concerns they had with our design. Below are the scenarios and tasks we presented to our colleagues during the high-fidelity design critique session, followed by key feedback and a discussion on how it will influence our future design iterations.

Scenario 2

An ITRR team member is collecting data to analyze and prepare a presentation for their leadership to express resident attitude data across the counties involved in the Pilot Community Grant Program. Deer Lodge(Anaconda) - Sweet Grass(Big Timber) - Powder River(Broadus)

Tasks:

  1. Navigate to the Resident Attitude Data dashboard.
  2. Select County from Pilot Community Grant Program.
  3. Create an infographic of selected resident attitude data.

Scenario 3

A Community Leader who is color blind wants to do some research on Mineral County and compare it to data gathered from Missoula County. They are looking to see where there are similarities and differences between small towns and large cities regarding State and Community Overcrowding.

Tasks:

  1. Navigate to the Resident Attitude Data dashboard.
  2. Select Mineral County.
  3. Select Comparison.
  4. Select Data Options.
  5. Change the color scheme.
  6. Export the graphs.
  7. Navigate back to the main page.

Scenario 4

A data visualization student is performing research based on their local community and looking for aesthetically pleasing visual graphs. They found the website through umt.edu webpage and investigate the following communties: Hill County(Havre) - Lake County(Ronan) - Garfield(Garfield) - Glacier county (Blackfeet Group)

Tasks:

  1. Navigate to the Resident Attitude Data dashboard.
  2. Select County.
  3. Look through graphs.
  4. Export the most interesting graph.
  5. Navigate to the homepage and repeat until satisfied.

Summary of feedback from in-class Design Crits

Below is a list of the most common feedback we received during our design crit session using our high-fidelity prototype.

  • Share/Export and download buttons need to be labelled clearly, there was an abundance of confusion during all critiques.
  • Font needs to be larger, it was difficult to read at times.
  • Labels need to be clearer, the site was not as intuitive as we had initially believed.
  • Color scheme and functionality overall was a success, most users enjoyed using the dashboard and gave positive feedback on aesthetics.
To illustrate how this feedback will impact future iterations of our design, we include a couple of annotated screen shots illustrating the problem, alongside a description of how we plan to resolve that particular issue going forward.
