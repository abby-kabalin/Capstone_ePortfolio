Storyboard (Low-fidelity prototype)

Using the information we gathered from our users and the resulting list of high-priority functional requirements, we created a low-fidelity prototype. This initial prototype, or storyboard, is essential because UI prototypes represent the project's design and program flow, including the interface's look, graphics, controls, and overall functionality. This is a critical part of the design process, allowing the development team to test the design's feasibility and see the interaction of implemented functionalities. Prototypes are also important for clients, as they can see the project's current development state, evaluate it, and provide feedback to ensure their requirements are being met.

Below are some annotated sketches of our low-fidelity prototype. They illustrate the dashboard's main pages and the user's interaction with them. The sketches show how the user navigates through the dashboard, views data, and interacts with the functions.

The first sketches show the main pages of the dashboard. The first sketch depicts the home page, where the user can see the main navigation and quick access to the most important features. The second sketch shows the community page, where the user can see the data specifics in their selected county. The third sketch shows the export page, where the user can export the data they have selected.

These sketches include a multitude of features, including:

  • A search bar in the upper right of the home page
  • A sidebar with all of the data toggles
  • Breadcrumbs in the upper left of the community page to assist with navigation
